How does ENaC close?

One of the proteins we study, ENaC.

ENaC

In the Baconguis lab, we're all interested one way or the other in learning something about the Epithelial Sodium Channel (ENaC). ENaC is a member of the ENaC/DEG family of ion channels, meaning it's highly sodium selective, trimeric, and blocked by the small molecule amiloride. Something interesting about (and unique to) ENaC is that it's gated by proteolysis. Two of the subunits contain peptides that hold the channel in a closed state. When they're removed, the channel opens and allows sodium to pass through.

My project is focused on how ENaC is negatively regulated. Since it can't be closed, the channel has to be internalized and degraded. Nedd4-2 (Nedd4L in humans) is the E3 ubiquitin ligase that initiates this process. I want to solve the structure of Nedd4-2 bound to ENaC via cryoEM. Although there are already crystal structures of each domain of Nedd4-2, my hope is that a structure of the two molecules in complex will teach us something about how the domains are put together. Also, Nedd4-2 might hold the damn TMD still so we can solve it ;)
